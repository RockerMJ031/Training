🧱 The wall = a Pareto OPTIMUM. You're stuck: you can't gain on one axis without losing on another. That's not failure — it's the limit of the old setup.

Almost every wall we live with was built from one thing — human hours. AI just moved nearly all of them. Find the wall you're stuck at — and move it with AI.
Read deeper · why this works & how to do it right
Why now: AI is a general-purpose upgrade — it pushes the whole frontier outward. What was "as good as it gets" becomes improvable again. So almost everything that's information-, cognition- or coordination-bound now has a fresh Pareto improvement waiting — and that's most of what Purple Ruler does. Prove you're at the frontier → you need an upgrade. The upgrade arrived.

Step ②

The catch: AI only opens the room — it's a Pareto improvement only if no one ends up worse. 03The flywheel — and the two Purple Ruler runs

A flywheel is brutal to start, but once spinning it carries its own momentum. Bezos pushed Amazon on one point — lower prices: cheaper → better experience → more traffic → more sellers → wider selection → lower cost → lower prices again. The lesson: pick the one point you push, and the loop amplifies the rest. Purple Ruler runs two coupled wheels — an internal engine and a commercial wheel, with AI as the gear between them.

● gold node = the one point you push: goal/data quality + skills built on the engine, proven outcomes on the business wheel.
⚙ AI is the gear. It makes the marginal cost of growth ≈ tokens — removing the "growth = proportionally more headcount & cost" brake that normally caps a service business.
The wheel throws off momentum — more delivered hours at a falling unit cost, without proportional headcount.

06What is a skill — and how to build & share one

A skill is a small, reusable instruction file — a ~15-line SKILL.md — that packages one repeatable job. Describe it once → anyone reruns it the same way. Any job you keep redoing → make it a skill.

≈ 98%less of MJ's time per ticket
Take pr-ticket-ops: it encodes 17 domain SOPs — the institutional know-how for every ticket type — and cross-checks the source tables (C4 · Ops-ST1) on every one of 4,590 tickets. By hand, applying 17 SOPs and checking every field on each ticket is days of expert work and easy to slip — practically impossible to do consistently at this scale. The skill does it every time, and a deeper, more consistent result than by hand.

⚠ Keep it small — don't let a skill grow into a "god-skill"
✘ Does too muchMany jobs fused into one → you can't test, reuse or share a part, and one change breaks everything. ✓ One job per skill.
✘ Stands on standing app keysAn always-on, org-wide app credential is a back door. ✓ Run on your user-token — acts as you, only what you're allowed.
✘ Everything in one giant fileAll steps + detail crammed into one SKILL.md, reloaded every run. ✓ Keep SKILL.md small — move detail to reference files, loaded on demand (that's why 17 SOPs is fine).

07The harness — what it is, and where it's heading

The model is the engine. The harness wraps around it to make it useful and safe — it feeds context, gives it tools, remembers across sessions, and enforces your rules. Claude Code itself is a harness.

CLAUDE.md
standing instructions
Read at the start of every conversation — our rules, terms, conventions. Write once = stop re-explaining.
MEMORY.md
cross-session memory
Records what it learned — gotchas, approaches you confirmed — and reuses them. Doesn't repeat mistakes.
hooks
guaranteed automation
Commands that run at set moments. CLAUDE.md is advice; a hook always fires — for things that must happen every time.
.env
local secrets · red line
Keys, tokens & passwords live in a local .env — the harness reads them; they never go in the chat. The chat is logged, so a pasted secret can leak.
Where it's heading → the two halves move in opposite directions
What the model will absorb — don't over-invest
scaffolding that patches what it can't do yet
  • prompt tricks ("CRITICAL!! you must…")
  • hand-built multi-step tool loops
  • RAG plumbing · thinking-budget config
  • verification & sub-agent orchestration glue
Next year's model ships this for free.
What only you can provide — invest here
the layer that plugs the AI into you & PR
  • your goals & standards (CLAUDE.md, your OKR, the spec)
  • connectors to your data & systems (lark-cli, your Base)
  • red lines & permissions — more autonomy needs tighter rails
  • human approval on high-stakes / irreversible actions
The model can get smarter forever — it still can't know your goals, data & rules.
The harness shrinks toward the model, and grows toward you. So stop hand-building cleverness the model will soon do itself — pour effort into goals, data & rules. That compounds and never goes obsolete.

macOS alt: brew install --cask claude-code · then run claude and log in.

② Install Lark CLI
npx @larksuite/cli install lark-cli config init
⚠ Security rule: the app requests a user token only — acts as you, with your permissions. No tenant token = no app-wide access.
③ Move your email into Lark

Migrate your mailbox to Lark Mail so Claude Code can triage & draft replies via lark-cli.

No IT team here — ask the AI for any step and it walks you through.

What's a "Lark App"? Installing the CLI sets up a small Lark app (an App ID the CLI authenticates through to reach Lark's APIs — your Base, messages, docs). We grant it a user token (u-) — it acts as you, limited to exactly what you're allowed to do. Never a tenant token (t-), which would act with org-wide app power. So everyone runs an app, but each one is scoped to one person — no shared back door.
📸 Stuck on any step? Screenshot the error and ask the AI — it walks you through the fix. (Then save that fix as your first note in CLAUDE.md.)
🔒 Non-negotiable · from next quarter
Every piece of AI work is checked by a human before it ships. No direct release.
You own what goes out — the model drafts, a human signs off. "Checked" = it matches the goal · every fact traces to source · you'd put your name on it. Zero exceptions on anything client · school · parent · finance · legal-facing. More AI speed makes the check more important, not less.
